Newest music craze:
Do-it-yourself political satire

Santa Fe, NM) A well-known political satirist says necessity has forced him to offer his latest parody song in a form that will require a little more active participation from listeners.
     Jim Terr, whose pointed parodies have been broadcast on the ABC, CBS, National Public Radio, NBC/Mutual/Westwood One, BBC, Voice of America and Pacifica networks and the Larry King, Jim Bohannon, G. Gordon Liddy, Jim Hightower, Thom Hartmann, Dr. Demento, and Mountain Stage radio programs, has more recently posted his musical satires on his website, www.BlueCanyonSatire.com, for free Web listening and free download by broadcasters.
      But, citing a lack of funds to produce and post his latest inspiration, Terr has simply posted his latest lyrics, “The Girl in the Picture”, a parody of the catchy 1966 Del Reeves trucker hit, “The Girl on the Billboard,” updated with reference to the female prison guard shown in the recent controversial photos of Iraqi prisoner abuse.
      Along with the lyrics, Terr has posted a sound clip of the original Reeves song, so that visitors can hear the original song and just “visualize” the new lyrics set to music. “People will have to participate a little more, and put the tune and the lyrics together for themselves, but that's probably a good thing,” Terr said.
      Terr's new lyric is unusual in that it is less partisan and finger-pointing than most of his song creations. “It kills me not to have a clear villain, but in this situation, I've taken a more ecumenical approach, leaving aside the issue of having swept this scandal under the rug until the photos were revealed,” he says.
     The first line of Reeves' original 1966 hit was “Who is the girl wearing nothing but a smile and a towel in the picture on the billboard in the field near the big old highway?” The first line of Terr's parody: “Who is the girl wearing nothing but a smile by the pile of Iraqis in the picture that they showed last week on 60 Minutes?”
      Terr's song satires and commentaries and Web campaigns are ordinarily funded by private contributions, but funds were simply not available on a timely basis to record the new song, Terr said. The BlueCanyonSatire website currently has a minor Internet and alternative radio hit with a parody of another c/w hit, George Jones' “She Thinks I Still Care.”
     This and three other current Terr parodies were all included in the CCCP Comedy Group program broadcast May 6 from the Pacifica Network flagship station, WBAI-FM in New York City.
