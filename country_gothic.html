"COUNTRY GOTHIC" feature film in development

Country Gothic   (c) 2003-2004 Jim Terr

 

A legendary but aging recording studio in a small town about 30 miles from Nashville, run by a legendary record producer, now in his 60s, more eccentric than he ever was (think Sam Phillips, Phil Spector, Jack Clement -- in the person of Harry Dean Stanton or Bruce Dern). With his incredibly beautiful daughter, a dedicated engineer and sometime back-up singer, they keep the studio going.

 

Based on his still-legendary status, he has attracted some top young hot singers to participate in recording a tribute album of some of his greatest hits. Each of them shows up to stay a few days in this rural town and record one of these songs, and each of them meets a horrible fate which in some way relates to the theme of the song. For instance:

 

Miller’s Cave: Gets lost and dies of starvation & exposure on a wilderness hike.

 

Running Bear: Drowns when car is washed away in a gully in a thunderstorm.

 

Big Bad John: Crushed by the caving-in roof of an abandoned building where he goes to meet someone.

 

The Hanging Tree: Hangs self in jail while waiting for arraignment on a local speeding charge.

 

Don’t Take Your Guns to Town: Shoots self in a hunting accident.

 

Wolverton Mountain: Which brings us to the basic story... As each recording session and coincidental death occur, we become more suspicious that the old man is jealous of the attentions paid by the singers to his beautiful daughter, and is somehow arranging for each of them to die. We are also led to suspect one or two of the singers themselves, perhaps to knock off the competition professionally or romantically.

 

The daughter sings some of the background vocals, and at the urging of one of the artists (strangely, to the producer/father’s irritation) she sings a song herself for the project, say Brenda Lee’s “Sweet Nothins”. At the climax, the singer of “Wolverton Mountain” and the producer/father kill each other in a struggle, as it becomes clear that it’s the daughter who’s been murdering these guys, as she and her father have a very twisted Oedipal relationship, and could never love anyone else, and will protect each other at all costs.

 

In keeping with horror tradition, neither of them is very conscious of their own psychology and actions. In the end, the daughter, unaware and unsuspected, survives, and continues operating the studio in loving memory of her father.
