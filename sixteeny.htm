LETTER IN SANTA FE NEW MEXICAN, OCTOBER 14, 2007

Senator Domenici has a chance to make a profound and impactful statement by what he chooses to do with the estimated two million dollars in campaign contributions he will not be using for their intended purpose.

As I suspect Senator Domenici would admit, if pressed, our current system of big-money donors and lobbyists perverts representative democracy by giving undue attention to the needs of Industry, at the expense of "us", who are unable to compete with those contributions, junkets, etc. And it distracts candidates and incumbents' energy and attention from governing and addressing the serious questions before us.

Senator Domenici could legally contribute his campaign funds to charity, and there are several public interest groups working to educate the public and to implement public campaign financing. Think of it: for six dollars per citizen, we could replace the contributions of Big Oil, Big Pharma, Big Tobacco, Big Arms Manufacturing, and all the other Big Lobbyists, and perhaps get our government back!

Anyone who doesn't think that special interest legislation passed recently for Big Pharma alone has cost each of us more than six dollars, hasn't done the math. I could gladly be assessed six dollars -- or twenty or whatever -- to get rid of this absurd cancer on our government.

Reviews on Senator Domenici's legacy are mixed, but this gesture would make even a skeptic like me think kindly of the retiring senator.

Jim Terr / Santa Fe
