These photos of Soviet and American people were taken (in 3-D "stereo") in 1982, distributed as "ViewMaster"-like stereo sets to many top U.S. government officials and agencies, published as calendars in 1985 and 1987 and given to all US Senators & Representatives, in an attempt to "humanize" Soviet people and emphasize our commonality  in those Cold War days.

These photos were widely viewed, and were credited by some with having hastened the end of the "Cold War" mentality of the time.

Half of the photos below are Americans; half are Soviets
