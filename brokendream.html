BROKEN DREAM CATCHER         Jim Terr © 2017

A woman I knew in the days of my youth
Used the bone of a cat, a bat’s wing, a hound’s tooth
To divine and entwine all the realms there before,
To make her aware of what might lie in store.

But one thing reserved mostly for her own use,
Which contained, as it were, the most powerful juice,
The most mojo and juju to make a life better
Was the spooky but beautiful Broken Dream Catcher.

One hung in the closet, one in the back hall,
Another where you hardly saw it at all.
She explained to me once how a totem so eerie
Could help make your life just a little more cheery.

We all have our doubts and our fears, she explained,
Our terrors and memories laden with pain,
Our wishes unfilled, our nightmares extreme,
All taken together, our much broken dreams.

Unlike good-luck charms and crocheted words of cheer
I suggest, placed maybe up there or here,
A Broken Dream Catcher to capture the gloom,
To keep it confined in one part of a room.

Thus leaving some space for your cheerier mind,
For thoughts and events of merrier kind.
A kingdom expander, a boundary stretcher,
The quiet but powerful Broken Dream Catcher.

So unto today I employ it routinely
To draw off the bad thoughts and sadness quite cleanly.
I know not what they might attract or repel
But I must say my life has been going quite well.

So if a small figure in black veil you see
When you visit a friend’s house for coffee or tea
Do not be afraid, it might be for the better
That not too far away is a Broken Dream Catcher.
