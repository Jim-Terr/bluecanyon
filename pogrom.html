El Pogróm

The forthcoming documentary, a historical re-imagining of an unimaginable event.

 

 

The early 1940s. Southwestern US. With the rest of the country distracted by war, old hostilities flare with an deadly intensity comparable to the pogroms of Europe -- and on a similar (relative) scale.

Not until now have these events come to light, events and memories so horrible that all involved - perpetrators and survivors - chose to forget.

Survivors, historians, and modern commentators (including modern demagogues and unrepentant participants) speak candidly of this most startling and forgotten episode in recent American history.

The forthcoming documentary, El Pogrom, a historical re-imagining of an unimaginable event. Shining a light on eternal passions and how they are eternally manipulated and directed toward inconceivable cruelty.

"Those whom we victimize, we fear." -Barbara Tuchman.

A pogrom is a form of riot directed against a particular group, whether ethnic, religious, or other,
and characterized by killings and destruction of their homes, businesses, and religious centers.
The term was originally used to denote extensive violence against Jews– either spontaneous or
premeditated – but in English it is also applied to similar incidents against other minority groups.
