News release November 30, 2009


“Wackwords” TV game show to debut on local TV

 

The locally-produced game show, “Wackwords”, will debut tomorrow (Tuesday, December 1) on Comcast Channel 16 in Santa Fe.

The program, a revival of the 1998 show “Wordsworth”, also hosted by local producer Jim Terr, is produced at Santa Fe Community College by the Community Television department (SFCTV), and features local guests.

The program is a variation on the parlor game called “Dictionary” or “Fictionary”, the board game called “Balderdash” and the newspaper feature called “Word Teaser,” and consists of contestants choosing very obscure words from the dictionary and creating a plausible definition to fool the other contestants into thinking it is the correct definition.

For instance, in the on-line sample of the old “Wordsworth” program, contestants defined the word “zander” as a monetary unit in Turkey; the wooly pile on large animals; to wander from country to country; and “to educate by terror or force.” (The correct definition is “a pike perch of central Europe”).

The on-line sample of the old “Wordsworth” program is on YouTube here.

The current “Wackwords” program will air and repeat three times weekly per episode, Tuesdays at 7:00 pm, Wednesdays at 9:30 am, and Fridays at 7:00 pm on Comcast Channel 16 in Santa Fe..

People interested in participating as guests can contact the program via its website, www.Wackwords.com .  The next program will tape Tuesday, December 15 in Santa Fe.

Sponsors and supporters of the program include The Cowgirl Barbeque in Santa Fe and Book Mountain Paperback Exchange in Santa Fe. 

The program will also air on stations in Albuquerque and Los Alamos.
