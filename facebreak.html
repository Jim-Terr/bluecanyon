THOUGHTS ON TAKING A BREAK FROM FACEBOOK

 

Not sure why I got off, it just sort of happened, you know? 
One day I didn't feel like going on and the next day, didn't feel compelled,
a week went by...didn't miss it.
    And then, magically, I had a lot more time for other things. 
       -Candace

 

I don't know how I let facebook weedle itself into my existence; so I certainly don't know how my existence and facebook became intertwined.   It seemed a way to connect with my friends, acquaintances, fellow animal advocates and protectors, and political activists.   It became a way to republish articles from animal groups, the New York Times editorials, Truthout, Common Dreams, Change.Org, Politico and so on. 
      It became a way to learn who was eating their baloney sandwich with a margharita at noon.  And a way of learning what Mercury was doing in the past, current or even in a future retrograde.  How had I ever lived without this information!  
      And then and then and then...I realized I was so busy reading, responding to and composing facebook messages I had no time to eat my own baloney sandwich!   So, goodbye facebook.  Hello baloney.  Or, should I say, godbye facebook goodbye baloney?
             Pat the Pitiless
