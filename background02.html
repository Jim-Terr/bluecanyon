Editor's comprehensive rant:
(blue underlines indicate links to information sources)

George W. Bush is not the problem.

George W. Bush is not the problem.

The audacity of this administration in threatening a "regime change" in Iraq or any other sovereign country it considers evil; the maintenance of the most expensive "defense" force in the world by far,* in the absence of threatening neighbors at or near our borders—these would be unthinkable without the willing acquiescence of an overwhelmingly complaisant and unquestioning Congress and press corps—and public.

The president's daily clench-jawed, hissing pronouncements about evil in the world and in modern life, backed up by your tax dollars and an ever-tightening view of free speech and civil liberties, would be absurd if not for the fawning millions of "dittoheads" and Christians co-opted by the right wing, and others of potential good will who sadly see this unexceptional good old boy as a clear, bright, trustworthy father figure and savior.

Those billions of dollars which, as Senator Everett Dirksen once said, start to add up to real money after a while, spent on "Star Wars" instead of something workable and important, such as actual human needs; the unlimited spending and effort put into making the United States the Master of both earth and space—this could be at least the subject of some debate, but the sad fact is that most Americans would probably vote to support the military-industrial-Congressional-administration complex on this question, if it were ever clearly posed as a question.

(Speaking of being hypnotized by irrelevant questions and news coverage, how is it that Martha Stewart's stock dealings take up so much air time—and even congressional hearings!—when Ken Lay and other corporate bandits wind up with millions of dollars after destroying their companies and their employees' futures?)

After all, a president who never had the curiosity to travel outside the US until he became president, who (like the vice-president and most of his cabinet) avoided military service and has therefore kept a pure and noble view of warfare and military conquest—this is an uncomplicated leader with an uncompromising world view that simple and accepting people can look up to.

(The perfect leader for a people who fall for TV spots like the ones running here in New Mexico, asking us to call our Rep. Heather Wilson to thank her for voting for a prescription drug bill so inadequate that Senate Republicans produced their own bill! The spots were sponsored by a group called the "United Seniors Association" [USA - get it?], which turns out to be [surprise!] a pharmaceutical industry front group! But don't get me started on media manipulation and "astroturf" phony grassroots groups and the people who fall for them. Propaganda is a whole 'nother story, albeit the key story of our time...)

Especially with a War on Terror in progress, and a war on Iraq in the offing, probably before Election Day. Who would abandon their leader in wartime? Certainly not you. Certainly not I. Certainly not Dan Rather. And certainly not the millions of dittoheads and co-opted Christians who bought into the "culture war" scenario long before it crystallized into a "War on Evil."

So 19 assholes pulled off a brilliantly-executed, vicious act of vandalism on September 11. (I'm not interested in their political arguments or gripes; I don't give them credit for anything beyond a rabid, blind fundamentalism). Touché! Now what? Presumably their best and brightest were killed in the event, thereby reducing, not increasing, the threat of terrorism. How does this translate into a sudden imminent threat that justifies spending hundreds of billions for every military action and civil liberties infringement that this crackpot administration can dream up?

How many billions are we willing to spend—are you willing to spend—to prevent one more terrorist act? Two more terrorist acts? Would any amount of money prevent one maniac from bringing an atomic bomb in a suitcase into New York City? How does the fact that 15 of the 19 terrorists came from Saudi Arabia, translate into an anti-terrorist war on Afghanistan, Iraq, Korea—anywhere but Saudi Arabia? Tracking terrorists is one thing, a needle-in-a-haystack enterprise but a worthy goal; cleansing this huge world of evil is a Roy Rogers fantasy. (Apologies to Roy Rogers!)

How does the CIA and FBI not being able to co-operate, not being able to "connect the dots", translate into spending 38 billion dollars (remember? those billions that add up to real money eventually…) to create a new agency to co-ordinate the anti-terrorism efforts that they couldn't handle? May I sell you a large bridge?

If the $148 billion (my rough estimate) spent so far on the "War on Terrorism" had resulted in, for instance, a reduction of the number of planes wandering over the White House in the past year, I'd be more convinced. (And yes, I'm all for defending the White House and whoever's inside it, even if it means shooting down an airliner or private plane that wanders too close by.) But no one has even been able to manage that so far, yet they (and we) are willing to spend tens of billions on a fanciful but profitable "Star Wars" experiment to shoot down speeding missiles in outer space, and to go along with faked tests to justify its continuation.

By the way, a word about the "co-opted Christians" I've referred to twice already. This is the source of my great sadness in this matter. How did these people of such great good will, with an ostensible desire to make the earth once again a garden, get hoodwinked into backing everything anti-Christian? War, hate, self-righteousness and vindictiveness, destruction of life in all its forms (excepting pre-natal). Can you say Ralph Reed? Can you say Rush Limbaugh? I'm not the first to observe that Jesus would be weeping right now. If only this tremendous potential for good could be harnessed for something constructive, such as a war on starvation and disease and tyranny.

What about stillness, gentility, simple fairness, spirit, humility, suffering and subsistence, indigenous ways and wisdom? What about Prairie Home Companion and Doc Watson? Do you think any great number of the testosterone-laden men and women in Washington who talk about getting government off your back just enough to con you into letting them pass a few thousand more laws each year to regulate your life and future for seven generations, are primarily concerned with these things?

Is there anything in the world, from your health to your love life to your imagination to your children to your DNA to the future of entire nations, that the unelected assemblage in the White House wouldn't like to control and commercialize if they could? Is there any activity, from incarceration to education to weapons manufacture, that they don't think could be handled more efficiently and ethically by the private sector? Is there any "white-collar" outrage, such as robbing wage-earners and retirees of their life savings, that they might consider as criminal as a kid holding up a 7-11?

I was fascinated years ago to observe a dog with one of those huge cones taped on this neck to prevent him scratching his ears, which had just been lopped off by the vet. He was yipping excitedly to see his master, happy to be rescued from this torture, unaware that it was his master who sent him to the vet in the first place!

Why does this remind me of people who voted for a president whose cabinet and whose concerns reflect the interests of oil and commerce and fundamentalism and self-righteousness, rather than any of the values and concerns they would like to see in their children? A president who has appointed wolves (industry executives and lobbyists) to guard the henhouse (us—his employers!) in virtually every area of health, safety, environment, worker and consumer interest? And who unashamedly promises to appoint more of the same, and more Supreme Court judges like the ones he says he admires most—Antonin Scalia and Clarence Thomas!

A president who takes advantage of every possible photo opportunity, whether it's forests or firefighters (always with a huge headline logo in the background to make sure we get the point), with no "journalists" daring to be impolite enough to point out that he's whacked a billion dollars from the firefighting budgets of the Departments of Interior and Agriculture! The "disconnect" between what this man says and what he does is frightening, almost as much as the unwillingness of the press to point it out and the unwillingness of the public to hear it.

Still, no, George W. Bush isn't the problem. Without us, without our need for simple answers and a seemingly noble leader who can put our many fears to rest, George W. Bush would be just another baseball fan in Texas, choking on pretzels, unknown except as the son of a former president whose "unfinished war" he's now determined to finish—with about 40 more of your billions, with the lives a few hundred thousand more Americans, Iraqis and their children, and against the advice of the leaders of every other nation in the world, who somehow lack his Chauncey Gardner-like insight.

A small price to pay to tie up some old, unfinished business.**

His quiet, gentle manner is somehow taken to indicate great wisdom by the rich and powerful.

 


Try this perspective . . .
(from an e-mail interview with author George Saunders)

Weekly Alibi (Albuquerque, NM) February 28, 2002

Obligatory disaster question: Do you think that the literary world will change/has changed in light of our new life during wartime? Have you seen any reflection of it in your students' work?

I think the literary world and even the real actual world is constantly changing even in light of the smaller, more pathetic disasters taking place on a regular basis. This disaster, like any disaster, will exaggerate our flaws as well as our virtues. It will bring out the best in some people, while others will make tons of money and/or use the disaster to push forward their moronic over-simplifications. Some will be propelled into a deeper spirituality, while others will take the disaster as confirmation of their pre-existing brutal, self-serving philosophies, or their callow, New Age, self-serving philosophies. Ego will continue to thrive. Crazy people will be made crazier, the weak and poor and voiceless will bear the brunt of the suffering, zealots of various stripes will take the disaster as proof that their historic opposition must be crushed, the real horror and clarity of that day will gradually and then more rapidly be used to move product and perform the subtle mythologizing of advertising, stunted versions of very real virtues (patriotism, heroism) will appear, as will (thankfully) amazing three-dimensional examples of same.

What are your literary plans for the future?

To rest after that last lofty and long-winded paragraph.

CRIMES, ETC.

From a column by Arthur Alpert in PRIME TIME Monthly (Albuquerque, NM) July, 2002

Washington was quick to decide that Osama bin Laden was not an adequate enemy. No, we must fight terrorism in general. No wait, let's make war on Evil itself! The purpose is to distract us from the Establishment's scary agenda.

I've been listing some of the items on that agenda, like escaping accountability for Sept. 11, turning energy policy over to the oil companies, avoiding rational discussion of national defense, reversing environmental safeguards, tabling health and other equity issues. Let's add subverting the US Constitution.

Immediately after Sept. 11, the Congress -- with one dissent -- passed the so-called Patriot act; this made it a snap for Big Brother to invade our privacy. But Washington was not satisfied.

Immediately following the recent revelation that nobody was "connecting the dots," did the Attorney-General fire somebody? Reorganize the FBI so that all the dots would come to one desk? No. He issued new guidelines making it easier still for the FBI to spy on us. At home, at church, on the Net, wherever. No evidence of a crime required.

A scary agenda, indeed. Where are the conservatives who are always telling us Government is too powerful? Where are the liberals? Afraid they'll be punished for standing on principle?

Where are the patriots?
