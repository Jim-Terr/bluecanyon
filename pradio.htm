HOW TO PROMOTE PUBLIC & COMMUNITY RADIO!

I love public and community radio -- absolutely couldn't live without them -- and therefore am very frustrated with what I see as tremendous missed opportunities to make them available to the wider audience which (trust me!) is not aware of them:

1. LOCAL COMMUNITY RADIO STATIONS: I see many beautiful ads which capture the essence of community radio – but only IF you're already aware of it, and therefore are a great waste of time. That great majority who AREN'T aware could be made aware of your station by one simple technique:

Look for specific upcoming programs or specials which will grab the attention of the general community, or large segments, for whatever reason. Advertise THAT, not the station in general.

Bringing people to a particular program, even once, will make them aware of the station and where it is on the dial!

Your local papers should be glad to promote your upcoming programs in news stories and in their calendar. After all, unlike commercial radio and TV, you are NOT competing with the newspaper for advertising dollars!

2. NATIONAL PUBLIC RADIO: NPR has a wonderfully full and active website and archives. I often hear a great news feature and then send out e-mails to friends suggesting they go hear it on the archives. WHAT IF NPR offered an e-mail sign-up specifically for listeners to be informed in advance of upcoming features on the next couple days' “Morning Edition”, “All Things Considered” , etc?

We could then inform more friends – including those who aren't aware of public and community radio (and most people ARE NOT) – in advance -- about upcoming items of interest. As above, getting people to tune in to public radio (and your station) once, will make them aware of it from then on!! (If ABC's Nightline can do this, why not NPR??)
