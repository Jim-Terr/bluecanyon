Trump Critic Denies He Hates Trump

   Special to The Houston Howler, April 13, 2025

Jim Terr's YouTube channel boasts almost three million views, and many of his recent posts are music videos about the shortcomings of Donald Trump - some of them quite dire and apocalyptic. For instance, "Reichstag Fire" flat-out predicts that Trump will use some pretense for a national emergency to declare martial law and cancel the upcoming elections - just as Hitler did!

Still, the New Mexico satirist declares in a recent post and fundraiser, "No, I don't hate Trump. I consider him, like Hitler and Stalin and other megalomaniacs, to be a damaged personality who has unfortunately gotten into a position of power where he can do a lot of damage, as we can see every day... I think he's still traumatized by his recent brush with imprisonment, and I think that's making him even more vicious than he already was."

Satirist Jim Terr

In numerous songs and essays, Terr has no hesitation in comparing Trump to the German dictator, in terms of his desire for full control of the country. "I think it's obvious that's his intention, and that he's put together a like-minded team who's very supportive of that, and the best use of my talents is to try to put a wedge between him and his supporters by creating satire that will reduce the enchantment he casts over his cult."

As a nationally-broadcast songwriter, jingle producer, and satirist, Terr has gained many kudos, but he regrets that he has yet to crack the "viral" video world of TikTok, X, and Instagram -- despite having as many as several hundred thousand views on some of his videos. "I think I need to hire a young consultant who knows that world better than I do," he says.

In addition to getting random donations for his work on occasional GoFundMe projects, he also has a very small base of Patreon patrons who "help me pay the bills," he says. His current project includes sending "wake-up" postcards to the GOP congresspeople who support Trump, something he has done before.

For a couple of award-winning documentaries he has produced, he has interviewed New Mexico Holocaust survivors and World War Two veterans, and for other videos he has interviewed prominent Trump critic (and ex-staff-member) Anthony Scaramucci, as well as humorist Dave Barry, NPR's Scott Simon, James Fallows, Tony Hillerman, Arianna Huffington, Charles Osgood, Valerie Plame, Mark Rudd, Ali MacGraw, Thom Hartmann and others, for other videos.

"But I'd like to interview Donald Trump if I ever had a chance," he says. "Not to give him more of a platform, but to try to draw out some interesting insights into this unusual character -- and perhaps even have a good influence on him."

You've got to give Terr one thing: He's not modest! 

   -J.S.

10+ surprising facts about the wildfire-ravaged Las Vegas

             (May 10, 2022, Houston Howler - All rights reserved)

Currently threatened by the largest forest fire in the US and the largest in New Mexico's history, Las Vegas, New Mexico is the "original" Las Vegas. Its history is surprising, and as a recent article in the New York Times pointed out, the local Hispanic culture is older than the United States itself!

Founded in 1835, more than a century before the other "Vegas,"  the population of this Spanish-American town on the Santa Fe Trail suddenly boomed in 1879, when the Santa Fe Railroad came to town, bringing businessmen, lawyers, hoodlums, and gamblers with it from Dodge City, KS, the previous rail head.

In fact, in his colorful book, The Wildest of the Wild West*, historian Howard Bryan says this Las Vegas was by far the roughest town in the West, by any measure including frequency of hangings! The most prosperous city in New Mexico during the period, it boasted the first and only Jewish synagogue, opera house, Masonic temple, and the finest hotels, a couple of which are newly renovated and still operating.

2. San Miguel County, in which this Las Vegas is located, is larger than the entire state of Delaware!

3. U.S. Senator Andrieus Jones, formerly mayor and U.S. Special Attorney of Las Vegas, was chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Woman Suffrage in 1919, when women were finally granted the right to vote, as codified in the 19th Amendment -- an event some say would never have happened without his support! He is buried in the local cemetery, and his home still stands in Las Vegas, one of 900 local buildings on the National Register of Historic Places -- more than any city in the West!

4. Some of the earliest silent films and westerns were shot in Las Vegas, including by famous actors/directors Tom Mix and Romaine Fielding. From the 1950s until the present time, many major films and TV shows have been shot there in full or in part, including Easy Rider, Red Dawn, A Fool for Love, No Country for Old Men, Longmire, Roswell, and hundreds of others. (See YouTube.com: "Las Vegas, New Mexico: America's Oldest Film Location.")

5. Las Vegas, NM, had telephone service in 1879, and was one of the very first cities in the US with long-distance telephone service!

6. Las Vegas' first radio station, KFUN, was the only station in the U.S. to buck World War II restrictions on broadcasting in a "foreign" language. According to Dr. Michael Sweeney, in his book Secrets of Victory, station owner Ernie Thwaites argued Spanish was a local language, not a "foreign" language, and resisted federal censorship efforts, even at the risk of losing his station and going to jail! (see website)

7. The Montezuma hot springs near this Las Vegas was the site of the first million-dollar hotel in the West. With bathhouses and a railroad spur running from town, the Montezuma Hotel hosted U.S. presidents and other dignitaries, and outlaws like Jesse James! It did not, however, welcome world heavyweight boxing champ Jack Johnson, who fought (and won) a title fight in Las Vegas in 1912.

The magnificent "Castle" is now the home of the American campus of United World College, with branches located around the world. Prince Charles spoke at its dedication in 1982, also attended by Cary Grant and financier/founder Armand Hammer, and other celebrities involved in the college's graduations have included Merv Griffin, "Dear Abby," Malcolm Forbes, and Queen Noor of Jordan. The Beach Boys played at the first graduation, in 1982.

8. President-to-be Teddy Roosevelt recruited his "Rough Riders" regiment for the Spanish-American War Cuban expedition primarily in Las Vegas, consisting mostly of local area cowboys. As governor of New York, he returned for their first annual reunion in a Las Vegas park; the reunion continued for many decades.

9. A forthcoming album called New Mexico Stew, to be released soon by Germany's prestigious Bear Family Records, was compiled in Las Vegas, and features a couple of nationally-known artists who recorded there. The long-time lead guitarist and arranger for the late, legendary jazz singer and pianist Nina Simone, Al Shackman, resides and occasionally performs in this Las Vegas.

10. Las Vegas, NM, was the site of an 1884 landmark case that established the right of Chinese Americans to participate in the American constitutional system. It was the first time an American court of last resort guaranteed non-Christian Chinese and other Asian Americans the right to testify in courts of law.

11. The nationally-used slogan, "Locally Owned," is trademarked in Las Vegas, NM, and the Trump spoof site and popular Facebook page, www.TrumpGov.us, also operates from there.

Monumental painting by legenday Texas artist resides peacefully in New Mexico town
Visiting filmmaker sheds little light on recent Rust shooting incident

Born in Houston in 1942, Andy Burns attained heights of visibility some artists only dream of. Yet today he maintains a low profile in an unpublicized location, his brilliant works the only evidence of a once-booming career.

Once called "a folksinger on paper," his portraits commemorate everyday laborers, workers, and hustlers. Burns himself has spent more time as a laborer than as a painter -- by choice.

He was never enchanted by the world of "art with a capital 'A'," though his work has been exhibited at the Museum of Fine Arts and the Fred Kline Gallery in Santa Fe, in Salida, Colorado, The Contemporary Arts Museum in Houston, The Galveston Arts Center, and featured in a four-page cover story in Texas Monthly.

His large (6-foot by 46") oil crayon painting of two Santa Fe bicycle racers was recently discovered in Las Vegas, New Mexico, "the original Las Vegas," an active art and movie-and-TV-filming location.

Burns said he sometimes toys with returning to painting, but for now is simply enjoying life and retirement.
