New Mexico man offers collection of feature film ideas, scripts, as an NFT*

 Jim Terr, a native and resident of Las Vegas, New Mexico, is a well-known, nationally-broadcast song satirist. His latest offering, "Google Me" (a parody of Paul McCartney's "Yesterday"), urges the listener to search for Terr on Google, which currently has over 6,000 references to his various projects -- and over 2.5 million views on his YouTube channel.

Among Terr's lesser-known endeavors is a collection of his feature film ideas and screenplays, which he published in a book in 2020 called Insert Machinations Here: Forty half-baked film ideas, and a few good ones. In a bid to join the cybercurrency craze of selling artworks as NFTs*, Terr has turned that collection into digital form, an audio book called Forty films in two hours.

Among the stories are synopses of completed scripts, The Plaza Murders, a historical crime thriller set in his movie-busy home town of Las Vegas, and Resting Comfortably, presented both as an old western and a modern drama (written with Alan Arkin in mind), about an old miner or a retired businessman, respectively, striking it rich in a casino, and taking his winnings "in kind" since the money owed is not available.

Other stories run the gamut from mysteries and horror to comedies and commentaries on media culture, with titles like American Skiffle, American Cannibals, The Dead Sea Mob, The Brill Building West, Country Gothic, Death Grip, The Quickening, Selling the Hitlers, Teddy Roosevelt Slept Here, The Tentacles, Vampire Daddy, and Zombie vs Vampire. Many of these projects include associated websites.

Terr hopes that purchase of this collection of film ideas will be part of an effort to get them made into films, which should qualify such a transaction as an investment for tax purposes. He says that the proliferation of new streaming services such as Netflix, Hulu, Amazon Prime, etc., has vastly expanded the market for new titles and projects.

Several of the stories are set in Las Vegas and other spots in New Mexico, which Terr hopes will ramp up the already considerable film activity there. Included in the collection are 15 stories reportedly suggested by silent film star/director Romaine Fielding, who lived and worked in Las Vegas from 1913 to 1915.


"All of Jim's stories are amazingly human." -Danny Rubin, writer, Groundhog Day.

"Jim is always at least one step ahead of his audience, the essential element of successful feature film-making." -Gerald Fried, Emmy-winning film composer (Roots, Star Trek, Stanley Kubrick)


 *A non-fungible token (NFT) is a non-interchangeable unit of data stored on a blockchain, a form of digital ledger. Types of NFT data units may be associated with digital files such as photos, videos, and audio [for purchase or investment]. Because each token is uniquely identifiable, NFTs differ from blockchain cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin. (Wikipedia)
